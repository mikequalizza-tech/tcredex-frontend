## What is an Investor on tCredex?

**Investors** are financial institutions that purchase tax credits in exchange for equity investment. This includes banks, insurance companies, corporate investors, and tax credit funds.

On tCredex, Investors find opportunities, evaluate deals, issue commitments, and deploy capital efficiently.

---

## Your Investor Dashboard

After logging in, your dashboard shows:

### Investment Overview

- Active commitments
- Pipeline value
- Portfolio summary

### Pipeline

Deals you're actively evaluating:
- Saved for review
- In evaluation
- Commitment issued
- In Closing Room

### Matches

New opportunities matching your criteria from AutoMatch.

### Portfolio

Closed investments and compliance tracking.

## Step 1: Complete Your Investor Profile

Your profile determines which opportunities AutoMatch surfaces.

### Basic Information

- **Organization Name**
- **Institution Type** - Bank, insurance, corporate, fund
- **Contact Information**

### Investment Parameters

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Min Investment | Smallest deal you'll consider |
| Max Investment | Largest single investment |
| Target Programs | NMTC, LIHTC, HTC, OZ, State |
| Yield Target | Expected return requirements |

### Geographic Preferences

- **Target States** - Where you want to invest
- **Assessment Areas** - CRA geography (for banks)
- **Metro Preference** - Urban, suburban, rural

### Deal Preferences

- **Preferred Sectors** - Healthcare, housing, manufacturing, etc.
- **Risk Tolerance** - Development stage preferences
- **Timeline** - Investment horizon expectations

### CRA Considerations (Banks)

- **CRA Pressure Level** - How important is CRA credit?
- **Assessment Area Priority** - Weight AA alignment?
- **Community Development Goals** - Specific objectives?

> **Tip:** Precise criteria = better matches. Update as priorities shift.

## Step 2: Find Opportunities

### AutoMatch (Recommended)

AutoMatch analyzes opportunities against your criteria:

1. Set parameters in your Investor Profile
2. Receive notifications when matching deals appear
3. Review from your dashboard

Match factors include:
- Credit type alignment
- Investment size fit
- Geographic match (especially CRA)
- Sector alignment
- Deal quality indicators

### Marketplace Browse

Browse all active opportunities:

1. Go to **Marketplace**
2. Filter by program, state, size
3. Review DealCards
4. Save interesting deals

### Program-Specific Browsing

| Program | What You'll See |
|---------|-----------------|
| NMTC | Deals with CDE involvement |
| LIHTC | Housing deals direct from Sponsors |
| HTC | Historic rehab deals |
| OZ | Opportunity Zone investments |
| State | State credit opportunities |

## Step 3: Evaluate Deals

### DealCard Contents

Each DealCard shows:

**Project Summary**
- Name, type, location
- Sponsor information
- CDE involvement (for NMTC)

**Credit Structure**
- Program type
- Credit amount
- Pricing expectations

**Financials**
- Total Project Cost
- Capital stack
- Sources and uses
- Investment required

**Impact Metrics**
- Jobs created/retained
- Community benefit
- CRA eligibility indicators

**Risk Factors**
- Sponsor track record
- Project readiness
- Timeline risk

**Documents**
- Due diligence materials

### CRA Evaluation

For bank investors, evaluate:
- Is project in assessment area?
- Does it meet community development purpose?
- Qualified investment characteristics?

## Step 4: Express Interest

When you find a deal worth pursuing:

### Save for Review

1. Click **Save** on the DealCard
2. Deal moves to your Pipeline
3. Add internal notes

### Request Information

1. Click **Request Info**
2. Specify due diligence needs
3. Sponsor/CDE receives notification

### Express Interest

1. Click **Express Interest**
2. Other parties see your interest
3. Communication unlocks when mutual

---

## Step 5: Enter Closing Room

When parties agree to proceed:

### Closing Room Access

- Full document repository opens
- Direct messaging with deal parties
- Checklist tracking begins

### Due Diligence Review

Standard due diligence includes:

| Category | Documents |
|----------|-----------|
| Environmental | Phase I, Phase II |
| Financial | Pro forma, appraisal, financials |
| Legal | Site control, org docs, title |
| Compliance | QALICB tests, certifications |
| Impact | Community benefit, support letters |

### Working with CDEs (NMTC Deals)

For NMTC transactions:
1. CDE has already issued LOI
2. Review CDE's structure and terms
3. Coordinate with CDE on commitment
4. CDE manages Sponsor relationship

### Direct Deals (LIHTC, HTC, OZ)

For non-NMTC transactions:
1. Work directly with Sponsor
2. Your commitment is the primary agreement
3. Negotiate terms directly

---

## Step 6: Issue Commitment

When underwriting is complete:

### Draft Commitment Letter

1. Go to **Issue Commitment** in Closing Room
2. Specify terms:
   - Investment amount
   - Credit type and rate
   - Pricing (cents per credit)
   - Key conditions
   - Target closing date

### Send Commitment

1. Review commitment preview
2. Attach supporting documents
3. Send to Sponsor (and CDE if NMTC)
4. Track responses

### Commitment Responses

| Response | Next Step |
|----------|-----------|
| Accepted | Proceed to closing |
| Countered | Review and negotiate |
| Declined | Deal ends or revise |

## Tips for Success

### Set Precise Criteria

The more specific your preferences, the better your matches. Vague criteria waste time.

### Prioritize CRA Alignment (Banks)

If CRA matters, weight assessment area heavily. System understands CRA geography.

### Respond Promptly

Good deals attract multiple investors. Fast response signals seriousness.

### Build CDE Relationships (NMTC)

For NMTC deals, strong CDE relationships matter. Evaluate CDE track record alongside deal quality.

### Diversify Your Pipeline

Don't rely on one deal. Maintain multiple active evaluations.
