## Program Comparison Matrix

| Program | Purpose | Established | Administered By | Credit Amount | Claim Period | Eligible Projects | Target Beneficiaries | Compliance Requirements |
|---------|---------|-------------|-----------------|---------------|--------------|-------------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **LIHTC** | Expand affordable rental housing | 1986 (Tax Reform Act) | IRS and State Housing Finance Agencies | 9% (competitive) or 4% (bond-financed) of eligible costs | 10 years (credits claimed annually) | Affordable rental housing | Households at or below 60% AMI | 15-year compliance period (often extended to 30+ years) |
| **NMTC** | Spur private investment in low-income and distressed communities | 2000 (Community Renewal Tax Relief Act) | U.S. Treasury's CDFI Fund | 39% of Qualified Equity Investment over 7 years (5% for first 3 years, 6% for last 4 years) | 7 years | Community facilities, businesses, mixed-use real estate in low-income census tracts | Residents of low-income census tracts | At least 85% of funds invested in qualified projects |
| **HTC** | Preserve and rehabilitate historic, income-producing buildings | 1976 (IRC Section 47) | National Park Service, IRS, State Historic Preservation Offices | 20% of Qualified Rehabilitation Expenditures | 5 years (4% annually) | Certified historic buildings used for income-producing purposes | Communities with historic assets | Must meet preservation standards; 5-year recapture risk |
| **Opportunity Zone** | Encourage long-term private investment in low-income and distressed communities | 2017 (Tax Cuts and Jobs Act) | U.S. Treasury | Deferral of capital gains taxes; step-up in basis of 10% after 5 years, 15% after 7 years; exclusion of gains on QOF investments held for 10+ years | Varies (tied to holding periods) | Real estate development, business investments, infrastructure | Census tracts nominated by states and certified by Treasury | Investments must be in designated Opportunity Zones |
| **Brownfield** | Incentivize cleanup and redevelopment of contaminated properties | Varies by state/local jurisdiction | State and local governments | Typically a percentage of eligible remediation and redevelopment costs | Varies | Properties with environmental contamination requiring cleanup | Developers and investors undertaking brownfield redevelopment | Must meet environmental cleanup standards |

## Strategic Layering and Use Cases

### Common Combinations

| Combination | Use Case |
|-------------|----------|
| **LIHTC + HTC** | Affordable housing in historic buildings (e.g., mill-to-housing conversions) |
| **LIHTC + NMTC** | Housing as part of larger community development projects (e.g., mixed-use with clinics) |
| **NMTC + HTC** | Redeveloping historic commercial or community facilities in distressed areas |
| **Opportunity Zones** | Can be layered with other credits to attract capital with deferred and excluded gains |
| **Brownfield Credits** | Often combined with NMTC or HTC to address environmental cleanup costs |

## Program Stacking Rules

### CANNOT Stack Together
| Program A | Program B | Reason |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| NMTC | LIHTC | Different program structures; NMTC for commercial/community, LIHTC for residential |

### CAN Stack Together
| Program A | Program B | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| LIHTC | HTC | Common for historic housing conversions |
| NMTC | HTC | Historic community/commercial facilities |
| NMTC | Brownfield | Environmental cleanup + community development |
| NMTC | OZ | Different capital sources, same project |
| HTC | OZ | Historic property in Opportunity Zone |
| Brownfield | Any | Usually addresses environmental costs separately |
